# What is Digital Governance?

## Introduction
Digital governance refers to the use of digital technologies by governments to improve public services, governance processes, and citizen engagement.

## Definition
Digital governance includes e-governance, digital public infrastructure, digital service delivery, and data-driven governance systems.

## Components
- E-Government services
- Digital identity systems
- Digital payments
- Open government data
- Online public services
- Digital records and databases

## Examples
- Aadhaar in India
- UPI digital payments
- Digital land records
- Online tax filing
- E-procurement systems

## Challenges
- Digital divide
- Privacy concerns
- Data security
- Accessibility
- Technology adoption
- Infrastructure issues

## Conclusion
Digital governance is transforming how governments function and deliver services. It is an important research area connecting technology, policy, and society.
